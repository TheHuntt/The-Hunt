Key I — The Wounded Soldier

A wound that moved

Somewhere in the first published case of a certain famous consulting detective, his companion — a war-wounded doctor, freshly returned from Afghanistan — is introduced. The very opening pages name his exact regiment: an army division identified by a number and a place.

Find that regiment's name in the original text (not a summary — the exact phrase matters). Take the first letter of each of its three words. That three-letter string is your cipher key.

Use it to decrypt this Vigenère cipher (A=0 convention, standard Vigenère table, repeating key):

OREFVQ

The decrypted word is a piece of period military slang for the very thing that wounded him — still used today in a slightly different sense.
